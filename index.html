I'm a Computer Science graduate passionate about coding, testing, and building real-world applications. I work with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Core Java, and the MERN stack. I’m known for being a responsible team player with a willingness to learn and a focus on real-world problem-solving.

My Story

Like many students stepping into the world of technology, I began with more questions than answers. I didn’t come from a background of coding competitions or early exposure to advanced tools. I started with basic HTML and Java, often confused and overwhelmed. There were moments when even simple errors made me doubt myself. But over time, I learned one important thing: you don’t have to know everything at once. What truly matters is consistency. I gave myself permission to be a beginner and focused on learning, building, breaking, and fixing things step by step.

Through personal projects like a weather app, a news aggregator, and even a full-stack GATE preparation platform, I not only learned new tools like React, Node.js, MongoDB, and manual testing practices. I learned patience, communication, and problem-solving. If I could give any advice to fellow learners, it would be this: don’t wait to be perfect before you start, your portfolio doesn’t need to be flawless, your first project doesn’t need to be fancy. Start with what you know, stay curious, keep improving. I'm still learning, still exploring, and building things not just to show skills, but to understand how far I’ve come and how much further I can go.
